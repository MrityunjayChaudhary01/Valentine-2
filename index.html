The beginning

Introduction

Okay, so this is how it all started.

I asked for tips on how to approach other girls and how to start conversations. I made it clear back then that I didn’t talk to you because you were the only girl I was talking to, but because you were a good friend of mine. I even asked you about what outfit to wear for the annual function, but in the end, I wore exactly what I wanted to wear on the last day.

In March, you got upset with me because I didn’t come to meet you and instead went to Parth. I hurt you without realizing how sensitive you can be. And maybe as karma, right after that, I ended up getting chicken pox.

Then came May. When you were about to return to Meerut, you were sharing snaps one day. I was teasing you, saying things like “ye nahi dhoya, uska kya?” and you replied to every single one of them. I think that was the day I truly started feeling something for you.

When I met you for the first time, I was unusually conscious about myself. Normally, I don’t care much about how I look around my friends—I just wear anything and show up. But that day was different. The moment I saw you, I thought, “Okay… I’m done for sure.”

Yes, I liked you—a lot. And maybe you felt it too. I remember waiting in the car for you, then we hung out at McDonald’s. And oh, before that, our plan got cancelled and delayed by a day. My mood was completely ruined. I didn’t have dinner that night, didn’t play any games, stayed up late just singing, listening to songs, and waiting for the next day. And then… came the first day.

Confesion

From the Heart

So… yeah. The truth is that I kind of liked you.

At first, it was because you were such a great friend. But when we met in May for the first time, I started admiring you—really looking at who you are. I wanted to understand why I was feeling this way, what it was about you that was making me fall.

No, I wasn’t checking you out. I was observing you. I brought those rings for the same reason—to see if you’d like them, to see how you’d react, to adore you in that moment and watch you feel happy. I knew you wouldn’t be expecting it, and you were surprised… and I think you were happy.

Moving on, I didn’t even realize that you noticed me getting disappointed when you changed. I really enjoyed playing badminton with you that day. And after that, I even asked Nitish to play badminton with me at the exact same place—just to relive that moment. But the court was occupied.

I just wanted to go back there… to feel that nostalgia of admiring you.

And then—boom—you were right there. What a moment that was for me. I intentionally ended the match early just so I could come back to you and return the shuttle. I was trying so hard not to make it obvious… but yeah, that was me.

Love

The meaning of Love

I think I loved you through your stories and your selflessness—for everyone, not just for me. Your innocence, your pure intentions, and your hypnotizing smile are the reasons I know I will always fall for you.

I swear, Poorvi, if someday I’m losing my mind and going completely crazy, just one simple smile of yours can fix everything. I can guarantee that. Because right now, that’s the only thing that truly matters to me.

You know how happy I feel when you smile—when you eat well, when you sleep properly. Your happiness makes me happy. I crave you the whole day, I find you in everything around me, and every time I get even the smallest chance, I end up smelling your hanky.

I don’t know how to explain this properly, but Poorvi, I admit that I’m not perfect for you. Yet you chose me—and that’s my pleasure. You chose to let me love you—that’s my pleasure. You still believe me every time when I say I won’t hurt you again, and even when I ask for another chance, you never hesitate to say yes and try again.

You being in my life… is a pleasure.

Lastly

My final words

Finally… Poorvi, no matter what happens—and I truly mean this—I will always admire you.

Even if I’m upset with you for any reason, whether something small or something big, I will always look into your eyes with the same love and affection.

Can I ask for one permission? Can I love you forever?

I know I’ve been too much at times. I crossed limits, I overdid things, and honestly, I’ve hurt you a lot lately. I don’t even know how to make up for all of that. But with this Valentine’s week—and Valentine’s day—I want things to feel more real, more ours.

I really want to understand how you feel. I want to make things right for you. I know you’ve sacrificed a lot because of me, and most of the time you never even mention it. There have been so many incidents where I made you feel terrible, and I hate myself for that.

I just hope that you won’t leave me. I hope you’ll trust me when I say that I will be better.

I promise you… I WILL BE BETTER. TRUST ME.
